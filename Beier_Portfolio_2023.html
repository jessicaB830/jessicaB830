Movie Theater Ticketing App
Self-Led Independent Project
Project Timespan: 1/23 - 2/23
Responsibilities: UX Research, Sketches, Wireframes, Prototypes
Technology: Figma
This was my personal project, which I started with the goal of making a movie theater ticket and snack preordering app that was accessible to as many groups as I could find that are often overlooked in design.

After my research, I was able to come up with a flow that included people of various abilities and interests, so that a wider variety of people could enjoy this theoretical movie theater experience.
Challenges
When starting this project, I wanted to make an movie theater ticketing app that accommodated the needs of all potential users, since such a wide variety of people are interested in seeing movies.

A challenge I faced achieving in this goal was predicting what people's priorities would be and what ordering of pages would be easiest for them to follow.
User Research
Since I was making this site as a self-led, independent project, my user research was limited to interviewing different people who I knew used mobile apps in the past, and conducting competitive audits.
User Interviews
After interviewing people I knew of various backgrounds, I came up with a few key groups besides general audiences whose needs I needed to keep in mind while designing the site.
Children

Many forms of entertainment are not accessible to young children, who have limited reading abilities and motor skills. Movies are an exception to that, and so any app that allows users to book movie tickets should also be as accessible for children as possible.
The Elderly

Movies are interesting to people of all ages, but mobile apps often are a pain for the elderly to use, since font size is more limited than on desktop computers, and since oftentimes they have less experience using mobile apps in general.
Individuals with Reading Difficulties

Movies are a great form of entertainment for those with reading difficulties, since movies allow them to enjoy longer stories in a way that is more accessible to them. Therefore, movie ticketing apps should be easy for them to use so that movies remain accessible to them.
Competitive Audit
After establishing the various users' needs, I then evaluated other movie ticketing apps to see what I could learn from them and improve upon.
Trends I Noticed
- Movie ticketing apps seem to mostly have similar, one-path layouts.
- Information about options for movies and snacks relied heavily upon text for most apps.
- Many movie apps had very bright yet low contrast color schemes that were sometimes hard to read.
- Text sizes were consistently small.
Research Conclusions
I organized my research into some common or predicted user problems, and some potential solutions to those problems.
Problems
Solutions
- While movie theaters themselves tend to be accommodating to many types people who are often overlooked groups in society, most mobile apps are not, which could mean a barrier to accessing movies for some.

- Move ticketing apps often rely heavily on textual information, which is nice for some that want more information, but detrimental for some who do not want cluttered text for various reasons.

- Movie ticketing apps often seem to prioritize having a fun, eye-catching UI over having basic readability in text.
- Limit amount of text used in the app as much as possible while still communicating main ideas, and simplify wording as much as possible.

- Increase text size as much as is reasonable, so that the app is as easy to read as possible.

- Have images in place of text wherever possible.

- Use a clean, easy to read UI over a bright, colorful UI to minimize confusion.

Making this site was a great opportunity for me to practice accommodating the needs of people who are often forgotten in mobile app design.

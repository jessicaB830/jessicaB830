DIY Craft Finder Responsive Site
Self-Led Independent Project
Project Timespan: 2/23 - 4/23
Responsibilities: UX Research, Sketches, Wireframes, Prototypes
Technology: Adobe XD
This was my personal project, which I started with the goal of making a theoretical DIY project finding site that was easy to use and helpful for crafters of all skill levels.

After my research, I was able to come up with a flow that helped beginner crafters, people with some experience crafting, and people looking for a challenge to all find new DIY project ideas.
Challenges
A challenge I had in making this project were defining a target audience, since such a wide variety of people like to make crafts, and there is such a wide variety of crafts to make.

In a similar vein, I also struggled to strike a balance between the ideas of focusing on one specific demographic of crafter, and sacrificing limiting the site's reach, and focusing on reaching as many crafters as possible, and sacrificing the site's individuality.
User Research
Since I was making this site as a self-led, independent project, my user research was limited to interviewing different people who I knew used DIY or craft idea sites in the past, and conducting competitive audits.
User Interviews
After my competitive audit, I decided that I would create a DIY crafting site that had a few ideas for several audience types, instead of focusing on just one type of potential user. So, after interviewing people I knew of various backgrounds, I came up with a few key groups whose needs I wanted to prioritize while designing the site.
Novice Crafters

May have never made their own crafts before, need easy projects and clear guidance.
Research Conclusions
I organized my research into some common or predicted user problems, and some potential solutions to those problems.
Problems
Solutions
- Existing DIY crafting sites seem to focus heavily on beginner or child crafters. There are few guides or projects for anyone else.

- Too many project ideas to scroll through may overwhelm the user with decision paralysis, and too few may leave them still wanting for ideas.

- While some experienced crafters may look for projects that require specific skillsets such as sewing or knitting, many sites for experienced crafters exclusively focus on one type of project and offer little variety in ideas.
- Have several tabs of projects sorted by difficulty, so that everyone can find a project that suits their skill level.

- Have only a page worth of ideas for each difficulty level, but update those pages periodically so that users can keep coming back for new ideas without being overwhelmed.

- In the experienced crafters tab, include ideas that require various skillsets, and some that do not require specific skillsets at all.

Making this site was a great opportunity for me to practice finding design solutions for many users with similar needs, but different preferences for presentation.

If I were to do this project again, I might test also dividing projects up by type of craft rather than difficulty, and test users experience with that version of the site versus the one I have based on difficulty level. Competitive Audit
To help me decide who the target audience for my DIY crafting site should be, I conducted a competitive audit.
Trends I Noticed
- Sites giving DIY craft ideas tend to either be formatted on one page in one large list, or in a archival type of flow.
- The majority of DIY craft sites tend to be geared exclusively towards beginners and children, OR experienced crafters. Rarely a mix of audiences.
- These pages looked best when they had a lot of pictures to guide users along, and not an overwhelming length of paragraphs.
